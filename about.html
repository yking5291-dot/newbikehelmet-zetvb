About.

We make boxes just for bike helmets. Our boxes don't just hold helmets, they keep them safe and look good, all while being kind to the planet.

We handle everything from the first idea to getting the boxes to you. We mix smart design with making sure things look good and being eco-friendly.

We think a box should do more than just contain. It should protect, tell what's inside, and make the helmet seem more valuable. That's why companies all over the world trust us with their helmet packaging.

🚴‍♂️ What We Do Best

We only do bike helmet boxes. That means we know exactly what's needed to keep helmets safe and looking great. A helmet has to show up perfect, ready to protect, and our boxes help make that happen.

Our designers and engineers work together to make boxes that both protect the helmet and show it off well. We balance how strong the box is with how much it weighs, so it's tough but doesn't waste material. We make each box fit the helmet perfectly, so it doesn't move around during shipping.

Whether it's a helmet for road biking, mountain biking, or just getting around town, our boxes work for all sorts of helmets.

🧩 All the Boxes You Need

We've got all kinds of bike helmet boxes:

Boxes for stores with bright, colorful printing.

Boxes with clear windows so you can see the helmet.

Fancy two-piece boxes for special helmets.

Boxes for shipping helmets bought online.

Boxes made from recycled stuff for brands that care about the planet.

We can change the size, shape, printing, and more on any box to fit your helmets and brand.

🎨 How We Design

Our design team is where the magic happens.
They use fancy computer programs to plan out the boxes before we make them. We start by getting to know the helmet, who's going to buy it, and how it will be sold.

Then, we turn that into box designs that:

Have extra strong spots to handle bumps.

Let air flow through.

Have clear windows made from plastic or plants.

Are easy to carry.

Fold up nicely for storage.

We also make sure the box shows off your brand. The colors, textures, and everything else are chosen to match your brand, whether it's a racing helmet or one for everyday use.

We want people to notice the box, like it, and remember it.

🏭 How We Make Them

We've got the machines and people to make great boxes.

That includes:

Printing that makes colors pop.

Cutting and folding machines that are super accurate.

Machines that glue and fold boxes fast.

Special coatings that make the boxes look and feel different.

Machines that add shiny details.

Machines that put clear windows in the boxes.

We use strong cardboard and paper to make sure the boxes are tough. We test everything to make sure it can handle being shipped.

🧾 Making Sure They're Good

We want every box to be perfect.
We check everything from the materials we use to the finished box.

We do things like:

Check the paper to make sure it's thick enough.

Make sure the colors are right.

Test the folds to make sure they're perfect.

Drop the boxes to make sure they're strong.

Look at the boxes closely to make sure they look good.

Our boxes can handle being shipped anywhere in the world, even with bumps and changes in temperature.

🌱 Being Good to the Earth

We think boxes should protect the planet, too.
We try to be as sustainable as possible.

We use recycled paper and inks that are made from plants.
We use coatings that are better for the environment and try to use as little material as possible.

Every box can be recycled, and we tell our customers to reuse them if they can.

We also:

Save energy when we make boxes

Recycle our waste

Use suppliers that are trying to be sustainable

Use printing that doesn't pollute

We think being sustainable is important for us and for the bike industry.

🌍 Where Our Boxes Go

Our boxes go all over the world.
We sell to helmet companies in Asia, Europe, America, and more.

We can ship small orders or big orders.
The boxes are shipped flat to save space and money. We also use special boxes to protect the helmet boxes during shipping.

We work with shipping companies to make sure everything arrives safely and on time.
